# TREM2

> [!abstract] In one line
> A microglial lipid/damage-sensing receptor; rare variants strongly raise [[Alzheimer's disease]] risk and it is central to [[disease-associated microglia]] biology.

## Overview
*TREM2* (Triggering Receptor Expressed on Myeloid cells 2) encodes a [[microglia]]-enriched receptor that senses lipids and damage signals and signals via DAP12. Loss-of-function variants (e.g., R47H) increase AD risk; complete loss causes Nasu-Hakola disease.

## Relevance to microglia
- Required for the transition to [[disease-associated microglia]] states in many models ([[gene relationship]], [[gene interaction]]).
- Modulates [[microglia activation]], survival, and phagocytosis around pathology.
- A leading [[therapeutic targets|therapeutic target]] (agonist antibodies); soluble TREM2 is a [[biomarkers|biomarker]].
