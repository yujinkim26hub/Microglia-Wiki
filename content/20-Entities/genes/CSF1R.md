# CSF1R

> [!abstract] In one line
> The colony-stimulating factor 1 receptor — a master regulator of [[microglia]] survival, maintenance, and activation, conserved across vertebrates and a key [[therapeutic targets|therapeutic node]].

## Overview
*CSF1R* encodes a receptor tyrosine kinase (ligands CSF1 and IL-34) required for the development, self-renewal, and survival of [[microglia]] and other myeloid cells. CSF1R inhibition depletes microglia (used experimentally and therapeutically); CSF1R signaling is also implicated in [[microglia activation|activation]] states.

## Relevance to microglia
- Conserved maintenance signal across species ([[shimizu-2025-microglia-evolution]]).
- Activation-associated microglial [[cell state|states]] depend on interferon and CSF1R signaling ([[chhatbar-2026-microglia-taxonomy]]).
- A tractable [[therapeutic targets|therapeutic target]] for modulating microglial number and state in neurological disease.
