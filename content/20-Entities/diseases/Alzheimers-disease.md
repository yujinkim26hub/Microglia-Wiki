# Alzheimer's disease

> [!abstract] In one line
> The most common neurodegenerative dementia, defined by amyloid-β plaques, tau tangles, and prominent microglial involvement.

## Overview
Alzheimer's disease (AD) is a progressive [[neurodegenerative disease]] characterized by extracellular amyloid-β plaques, intracellular tau neurofibrillary tangles, synapse and neuron loss, and chronic [[neuroinflammation]]. Genetics highlight strong myeloid/microglial risk components.

## Relevance to microglia
- Many AD risk genes are microglia-enriched ([[gene relationship]]): *TREM2*, *APOE*, *BIN1*, *CR1*, *MS4A*, *SPI1*.
- [[disease-associated microglia]] (DAM) cluster around plaques; [[microglia activation]] and [[neuroinflammation]] are core features.
- A major arena for microglial [[therapeutic targets]] (TREM2 agonists, complement, NLRP3) and [[biomarkers]] (sTREM2, TSPO-PET).
