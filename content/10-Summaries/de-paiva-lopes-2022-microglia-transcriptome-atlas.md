## Core question
What drives variation in the human [[microglia|microglial]] transcriptome across [[brain region|brain regions]], [[development stage|aging]], sex, and disease, and which genetic variants regulate microglial gene expression and splicing?

## Prior studies
Microglia carry many [[neurodegenerative disease]] risk genes, but most prior microglial transcriptomics came from limited samples, single regions, or mouse models.

## Limitations of prior studies
Few resources captured [[brain region|regional]] and [[development stage|age]]-related microglial heterogeneity at scale in humans, or mapped microglia-specific regulatory genetics.

## What this study did
Generated and analyzed a large bulk-transcriptomic atlas of primary human microglia across multiple brain regions and donors, characterizing heterogeneity and mapping molecular QTLs.

## Main results of this study
- Profiled **255 primary human microglial samples** from **100 individuals**, across multiple [[brain region|brain regions]].
- Identified [[brain region]], age, sex, and disease state as major sources of microglial transcriptomic [[cell state|heterogeneity]].
- Molecular QTL analysis (expression and splicing QTLs) implicated variants affecting microglial gene expression/splicing, linking them to neurological disease ([[gene relationship]]).
