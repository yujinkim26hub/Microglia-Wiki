## Core question
Can a large cross-cohort meta-analysis of single-nucleus eQTLs resolve, by [[cell state|brain cell type/subtype]], which genes mediate genetic risk for brain disorders ([[gene relationship]])?

## Prior studies
Most brain-disorder risk variants are noncoding eQTLs, but bulk-tissue/cell-line eQTLs miss the brain's cellular diversity.

## Limitations of prior studies
Bulk eQTLs blur cell-type-specific effects; single-cohort snRNA-seq eQTL studies are underpowered.

## What this study did
Integrated snRNA-seq + genotype data across four cohorts ("SingleBrain") to map cis- and trans-eQTLs across brain cell types/subtypes, then linked them to disease via colocalization and Mendelian randomization.

## Main results of this study
- Meta-analysis of **~5.8 million nuclei from 983 individuals** (European ancestry) across **four cohorts**.
- Mapped **cis- and trans-eQTLs** across major brain cell types/subtypes, including disease- and sex-specific eQTLs.
- Colocalization + Mendelian randomization nominated genes mediating [[neurodegenerative disease|neurological]] and [[neuropsychiatric disease|psychiatric]] disease risk, including [[microglia|microglial]] effects.
