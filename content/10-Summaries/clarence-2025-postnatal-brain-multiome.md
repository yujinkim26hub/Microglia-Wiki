## Core question
Which cell-type-specific regulators drive human **postnatal** brain [[development stage|development]], and how do they relate to genetic risk for brain disorders ([[gene interaction]], [[gene relationship]])?

## Prior studies
Developmental brain atlases focused largely on prenatal stages; postnatal multiomic regulatory maps were limited.

## Limitations of prior studies
Sparse joint expression+chromatin data across postnatal stages and regions; weak linkage of GWAS loci to developmental cell types.

## What this study did
Jointly profiled gene expression and chromatin accessibility in single nuclei across postnatal stages, brain regions, and donors, then built enhancer-based gene regulatory networks and interpreted disorder GWAS loci.

## Main results of this study
- Joint snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq of **101,924 single nuclei**, **4 brain regions**, **10 donors**, across **5 postnatal stages** (infancy → late adulthood).
- Built enhancer-based **gene regulatory networks** ([[gene interaction]]) identifying cell-type-specific developmental regulators.
- Connected **2,318 cell-specific loci to 1,149 genes** (41% of loci) for **10 brain disorders**; highlighted **55 genes** affecting multiple phenotypes ([[gene relationship]]).
