## Core question
Can the substantial diversity of microglial/myeloid [[cell state|states]] across development, homeostasis, and disease be organized into a single comparable, stratifiable taxonomy spanning mouse and human pathologies?

## Prior studies
Single-cell studies have revealed extensive microglial heterogeneity and disease-associated programs (e.g., [[disease-associated microglia|DAM]]), each typically defined within one disease or model.

## Limitations of prior studies
No common framework existed to **compare and stratify** microglial states across contexts, species, and disorders — state labels were study-specific and not interoperable.

## What this study did
The authors single-cell RNA-sequenced >1,000,000 CNS cells from >30 physiological and pathological conditions to build a unified myeloid-state atlas, added [[spatial transcriptomics]] to localize states in tissue, and used in vivo perturbations to test signaling dependencies of activation states.

## Main results of this study
- An atlas of CNS myeloid cell states from **>1 million cells** across **>30 conditions** (mouse models + human disease).
- A taxonomy of **27 superclusters and 192 clusters**, prevalent across diseases and **largely conserved**.
- [[spatial transcriptomics|Spatial]] mapping of how immune-cell states are organized in tissue and interact with their local cellular environment ([[cell-cell interaction]]).
- In vivo perturbation showed **activation-associated microglial states depend on interferon and [[CSF1R|colony-stimulating factor 1 receptor (CSF1R)]] signaling**.

## Main findings
- Microglial/myeloid states across the CNS can be unified into a **conserved, hierarchical taxonomy** spanning species and pathologies.
- States are **spatially organized** and interact with local niches.
- Activation states are **interferon- and [[CSF1R]]-dependent**, nominating tractable signaling nodes.

## Relevance to microglia and neurological disease
Provides a shared, spatially aware reference taxonomy for CNS immune [[cell state|states]] — a [[brain single-cell atlas]]-type framework for placing and comparing microglial states (including [[disease-associated microglia]]) across [[neurodegenerative disease|neurodegenerative]] and other disorders, and surfaces [[therapeutic targets|targetable]] signaling ([[CSF1R]], interferon) for [[microglia activation]].
