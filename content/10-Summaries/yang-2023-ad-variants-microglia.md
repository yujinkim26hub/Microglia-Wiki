## Core question
Which noncoding [[Alzheimer's disease]] risk variants act through [[microglia|microglial]] regulatory elements, and what are their target genes and functional effects ([[gene relationship]])?

## Prior studies
AD heritability concentrates in noncoding regions enriched in microglial regulatory elements, but linking specific variants to target genes and function was incomplete.

## Limitations of prior studies
Bulk or non-microglial maps and association-only analyses could not establish causal variant→gene→function chains in microglia.

## What this study did
Built a microglia-specific 3D epigenome annotation, prioritized AD risk variants in candidate cis-regulatory elements (cCREs), and tested variant→gene links functionally with single-cell CRISPRi in microglia.

## Main results of this study
- Microglial cCREs showed the **strongest enrichment for AD heritability** among brain cell types.
- Prioritized **308 previously unreported AD risk variants at 181 cCREs** via microglia-specific 3D epigenome annotation ([[gene interaction]]).
- Linked variants to targets by **single-cell CRISPRi**; e.g. **rs7922621** controls **TSPAN14**, affecting cell-surface ADAM10 and **soluble [[TREM2]] (sTREM2) shedding** ([[biomarkers]]).
