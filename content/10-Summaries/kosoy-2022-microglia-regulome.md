## Core question
How does genetic variation shape the human [[microglia|microglial]] regulome (expression + chromatin accessibility), and can microglia-specific regulation refine [[Alzheimer's disease]] risk loci?

## Prior studies
AD GWAS implicate many noncoding loci enriched in myeloid/microglial regulatory elements, but causal genes and mechanisms in microglia were often unresolved ([[gene relationship]]).

## Limitations of prior studies
Bulk-tissue or non-microglial regulatory maps cannot pinpoint microglia-specific enhancer–promoter effects underlying AD risk.

## What this study did
Profiled transcriptome and chromatin accessibility in primary human microglia from many donors to map genetically driven regulatory variation and enhancer–promoter interactions, then fine-mapped AD loci.

## Main results of this study
- Transcriptome + chromatin-accessibility profiling of primary human microglia from **150 donors**; mapped cell-specific enhancer–promoter (E–P) interactions ([[gene interaction]]).
- Integrative fine-mapping gave putative regulatory mechanisms for **21 AD risk loci**, refining **18 to a single gene**, including **3 new candidate genes (KCNN4, FIBP, LRRC25)**.
- Transcription-factor regulatory networks captured AD risk and nominated **[[SPI1]] as a key regulator** of microglial expression and AD risk.
