## Core question
How do brain [[cell state|cell populations]] and their molecular signatures change with [[development stage|aging]] and [[Alzheimer's disease]] pathogenesis across human and mouse brains?

## Prior studies
Single-cell brain atlases existed, but scalable capture of **rare** cell types and age-dependent dynamics was limited.

## Limitations of prior studies
Cost/throughput limited detection of rare populations and cross-species, age-resolved comparison.

## What this study did
Developed EasySci, a high-throughput single-cell combinatorial-indexing method, and profiled large numbers of transcriptomes and chromatin-accessibility profiles across aging mouse brains and human brains to chart aging/AD-associated dynamics.

## Main results of this study
- **EasySci** combinatorial-indexing strategy; ~**1.5 million single-cell transcriptomes** + ~**400,000 chromatin-accessibility profiles** across mouse brains; **>300 cell subtypes**.
- Identified rare cell types expanded/depleted with [[development stage|aging]]; examined cell-type responses to AD-linked genetic alterations.
- Profiled **118,240 human brain single-cell transcriptomes** to find cell- and [[brain region|region]]-specific changes tied to AD pathogenesis (incl. [[microglia]]).
