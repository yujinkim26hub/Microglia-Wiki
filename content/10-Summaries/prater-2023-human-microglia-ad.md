## Core question
Which [[microglia|microglial]] transcriptional [[cell state|states]] are altered in human [[Alzheimer's disease]] brain at single-nucleus resolution?

## Prior studies
[[disease-associated microglia|DAM]] and related states were defined largely in mouse models; human microglial states in AD were less resolved.

## Limitations of prior studies
Human snRNA-seq microglial states are sparse (microglia are a small nuclear fraction), and prior human work captured limited microglial diversity.

## What this study did
Single-nucleus RNA sequencing of microglia from aged human dorsolateral prefrontal cortex comparing AD cases and controls, defining microglial subpopulations and their disease association.

## Main results of this study
- snRNA-seq of microglia nuclei from **12 AD and 10 control** human dorsolateral prefrontal cortices (aged >60).
- Identified **10 distinct microglia clusters**, including homeostatic, senescent, and inflammatory phenotypes plus additional states.
- Specific microglial phenotypes were **more prevalent in AD** than controls.
