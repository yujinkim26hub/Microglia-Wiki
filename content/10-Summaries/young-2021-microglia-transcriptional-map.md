## Core question
What shapes transcriptional [[cell state|heterogeneity]] in human [[microglia]], and which genetic variants have microglia-specific regulatory effects ([[gene relationship|eQTLs]])?

## Prior studies
Microglial eQTLs were hard to obtain because microglia are difficult to isolate fresh and are under-represented in bulk-brain datasets.

## Limitations of prior studies
Bulk-brain eQTLs miss microglia-specific regulation; few datasets captured human microglial heterogeneity at scale.

## What this study did
Profiled primary human microglia isolated during neurosurgery using single-cell and bulk RNA-seq, mapped microglia eQTLs, and fine-mapped an AD candidate variant in an iPSC macrophage model.

## Main results of this study
- Primary human microglia from **141 patients** (neurosurgery), via single-cell and bulk RNA-seq.
- Characterized how age, sex, and clinical pathology shape microglial gene expression and [[cell state|heterogeneity]].
- Mapped microglia-specific **eQTLs** ([[gene relationship]]); fine-mapped a candidate causal [[Alzheimer's disease]] variant at the **[[BIN1]]** locus using an iPSC-derived macrophage model.
