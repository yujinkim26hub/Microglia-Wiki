## Core question
Which genes and variants drive [[Alzheimer's disease]] risk, as resolved by an updated GWAS meta-analysis, statistical fine-mapping, and integrative gene prioritization ([[gene relationship]])?

## Prior studies
Prior AD GWAS identified many loci but causal variants/genes remained uncertain, especially at noncoding loci enriched in [[microglia|microglial]] regulation.

## Limitations of prior studies
Association signals alone rarely pinpoint causal SNPs/genes; integration with functional/QTL data was incomplete.

## What this study did
Performed an expanded AD GWAS meta-analysis, applied multiple SNP fine-mapping methods, and integrated colocalization, networks, and tissue expression to prioritize causal genes.

## Main results of this study
- Identified **37 risk loci**, including new associations near **CCDC6, TSPAN14, NCK2, SPRED2**.
- Three fine-mapping methods nominated **21 SNPs with >50% causal probability** each.
- Colocalization across **109 eQTL datasets** + network/tissue prioritization converged on likely causal genes (new genes plus **BIN1, APH1B, PTK2B, PILRA, CASS4**) — many [[microglia|microglial]] ([[gene relationship]]).
