## Overview
Single-cell/nucleus RNA-seq has become central to neurological-disease research, revealing how cell-type composition and [[cell state|state]] distributions shift in disease and enabling cross-disease, cross-species [[brain single-cell atlas|atlases]] and [[disease-associated microglia|DAM]]-type state definitions.

## Key ideas
- Disease often shifts the *distribution* of [[cell state|states]] rather than adding new cell types.
- Pan-disease [[brain single-cell atlas|atlases]] enable comparison and stratification across disorders.
- [[spatial transcriptomics|Spatial]] context links states to pathology and [[cell-cell interaction|niche interactions]].

## Strengths & limitations
- **Strengths:** state-resolved, cross-condition comparison, mechanistic perturbation when paired with in vivo models.
- **Limitations:** human tissue access, batch/cohort effects, snapshot (not longitudinal) data.
