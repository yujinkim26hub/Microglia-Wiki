# Brain single-cell atlas

> [!abstract] In one line
> Reference maps of brain cell types and [[cell state|states]] across [[brain region|regions]], [[development stage|ages]], species, and disease.

## Overview
Brain single-cell/nucleus atlases (e.g., human brain cell atlases, Allen Brain Cell Atlas, disease cohorts) provide reference taxonomies of cell types and states, against which new datasets and [[microglia|microglial]] states (including [[disease-associated microglia]]) can be mapped and compared.

## Key ideas
- Enable label transfer and cross-study harmonization of [[cell state]] definitions.
- Span [[brain region|regional]] and [[development stage|developmental]] axes.
- Built from [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]], increasingly with [[spatial transcriptomics]] and [[multi-omics]].

## Strengths & limitations
- **Strengths:** shared references; comparability; discovery of rare states.
- **Limitations:** batch/cohort biases; evolving annotations; access/ethics for human tissue.
