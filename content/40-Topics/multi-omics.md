# Multi-omics

> [!abstract] In one line
> Integration of multiple molecular layers (RNA, chromatin, protein, epigenome) to build a fuller picture of [[microglia|microglial]] biology.

## Overview
Multi-omics combines modalities — transcriptome ([[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]]), chromatin accessibility (scATAC-seq), DNA methylation, proteomics (e.g., CITE-seq), and [[spatial transcriptomics]] — jointly or via integration, strengthening inference of [[cell state|states]], [[gene interaction|regulatory networks]], and [[gene relationship|gene–disease links]].

## Key ideas
- Joint profiling (e.g., 10x Multiome) links accessibility to expression in the same cell.
- Strengthens causal interpretation of [[gene relationship|risk genes]] and [[therapeutic targets]].
- Supports robust [[biomarkers]] discovery.

## Strengths & limitations
- **Strengths:** cross-modal validation; mechanistic depth.
- **Limitations:** integration complexity; batch effects; cost and sparsity.
