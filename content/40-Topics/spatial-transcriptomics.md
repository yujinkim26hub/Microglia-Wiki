# Spatial transcriptomics

> [!abstract] In one line
> Methods that measure gene expression while preserving tissue location — placing [[microglia|microglial]] [[cell state|states]] in spatial context.

## Overview
Spatial transcriptomics (e.g., Visium, MERFISH, Xenium, Slide-seq) maps transcripts to tissue coordinates, revealing where [[disease-associated microglia]] sit relative to pathology (e.g., amyloid plaques) and enabling spatially grounded [[cell-cell interaction]] analysis.

## Key ideas
- Bridges the spatial gap left by [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]].
- Imaging-based methods give subcellular resolution but limited gene panels; sequencing-based give whole-transcriptome but coarser resolution.
- Critical for localizing [[neuroinflammation]] and microglial niches by [[brain region]].

## Strengths & limitations
- **Strengths:** spatial context; niche and neighborhood analysis.
- **Limitations:** resolution vs. gene-panel trade-offs; segmentation challenges; cost.
