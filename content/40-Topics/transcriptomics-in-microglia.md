# Transcriptomics in microglia

## Overview
Transcriptomics is the dominant lens on microglial biology: bulk RNA-seq of purified microglia, [[single-cell transcriptomics]] ([[snRNA-seq]] / [[scRNA-seq]]), and [[spatial transcriptomics]] together resolve homeostatic vs. [[disease-associated microglia|disease-associated]] programs, [[microglia activation|activation]] states, and their tissue organization.

## Key ideas
- Purified-microglia bulk RNA-seq gives large effect sizes and isoform-level detail but no [[cell state|state]] resolution.
- Single-cell/nucleus methods resolve states and build [[brain single-cell atlas|taxonomies]].
- Spatial methods place states relative to pathology; [[multi-omics]] adds regulatory depth.
- Outputs: [[differential gene expression|DEGs]] and [[gene expression signature|signatures]] feeding [[biomarkers]] and [[therapeutic targets]].

## Strengths & limitations
- **Strengths:** unbiased, scalable, increasingly multi-modal.
- **Limitations:** dissociation artifacts, dropout, region/species differences (see [[shimizu-2025-microglia-evolution]]).
