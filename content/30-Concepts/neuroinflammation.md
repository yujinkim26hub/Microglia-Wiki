# Neuroinflammation

> [!abstract] In one line
> The inflammatory response within the CNS, driven largely by [[microglia]] and astrocytes, that can be protective or pathological.

## Definition
Neuroinflammation is the activation of CNS innate immunity — cytokine/chemokine release, microglial and astrocytic reactivity, and immune-cell recruitment — in response to injury, infection, protein aggregates, or chronic stress. Acute responses aid repair; chronic, dysregulated responses contribute to tissue damage.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Chronic neuroinflammation is a shared feature of [[Alzheimer's disease]] and other [[neurodegenerative disease|neurodegenerative]], [[neurodevelopmental disease|neurodevelopmental]], and [[neuropsychiatric disease|neuropsychiatric]] conditions, and a major source of [[biomarkers]] and [[therapeutic targets]].

## Key points
- Tightly linked to [[microglia activation]] and emergence of [[disease-associated microglia]] states.
- Mediated through [[cell-cell interaction]] (e.g., complement, cytokines) among glia, neurons, and peripheral immune cells.
- Quantified via [[multi-omics]], [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]], and [[spatial transcriptomics]].
