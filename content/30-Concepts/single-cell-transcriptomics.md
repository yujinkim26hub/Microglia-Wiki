## Definition
Single-cell transcriptomics profiles RNA in individual cells ([[scRNA-seq]]) or nuclei ([[snRNA-seq]]), enabling unbiased discovery of cell types and [[cell state|states]] and of how their proportions shift in disease. It is the cell-resolved branch of [[transcriptomics]].

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
It is the workhorse for resolving [[microglia|microglial]] heterogeneity and for defining [[disease-associated microglia]], mapping [[neuroinflammation]], and assembling the [[brain single-cell atlas|brain cell atlases]] used across neurological disease.

## Key points
- Implemented via [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq|snRNA-seq / scRNA-seq]]; snRNA-seq suits frozen human brain and reduces dissociation artifacts.
- Yields [[differential gene expression]] and [[gene expression signature|signatures]] per state.
- Gains tissue context when paired with [[spatial transcriptomics]] and depth when paired with [[multi-omics]].
