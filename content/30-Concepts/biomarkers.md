# Biomarkers

> [!abstract] In one line
> Measurable molecular or imaging indicators of disease state, progression, or treatment response.

## Definition
Biomarkers are objectively measured indicators — fluid (CSF/plasma), imaging (PET/MRI), or molecular ([[multi-omics]]) — used for diagnosis, staging, and monitoring. Microglia-related examples include sTREM2, and PET ligands targeting TSPO.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Microglial and [[neuroinflammation|neuroinflammatory]] biomarkers (e.g., sTREM2, TSPO-PET) help track [[disease-associated microglia|disease-associated microglial]] activity in [[Alzheimer's disease]] and other [[neurodegenerative disease|neurodegenerative]] conditions, and to evaluate [[therapeutic targets]].

## Key points
- Derived from fluids, imaging, or omics signatures.
- Single-cell-derived [[cell state]] signatures are an emerging biomarker source.
