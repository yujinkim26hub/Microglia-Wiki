# Transcriptomics

> [!warning] Seed note
> This is a **seed page** that needs paper-backed refinement. It will be expanded and cited as papers are ingested.

> [!abstract] In one line
> The study of the full set of RNA transcripts in a cell, tissue, or system — the molecular readout underlying most modern [[microglia]] research.

## Definition
Transcriptomics measures the abundance (and increasingly the location and modification) of RNA transcripts to describe what genes a cell is expressing. It spans bulk RNA-seq, [[single-cell transcriptomics]] ([[snRNA-seq]] / [[scRNA-seq]]), [[spatial transcriptomics]], and integration with other layers in [[multi-omics]].

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Transcriptomics is the primary lens through which microglial heterogeneity, [[cell state|states]], and disease responses are defined. It underpins discovery of [[disease-associated microglia]], characterization of [[neuroinflammation]], and construction of the [[brain single-cell atlas|brain cell atlases]] used across [[neurodegenerative disease|neurodegenerative]], [[neurodevelopmental disease|neurodevelopmental]], and [[neuropsychiatric disease|neuropsychiatric]] conditions.

## Key points
- Levels: bulk → single-cell/nucleus → spatial → multi-omic integration.
- Outputs feed [[differential gene expression]], [[gene expression signature|expression signatures]], and the broader transcriptional landscape.
- The dominant data type catalogued in this wiki.
