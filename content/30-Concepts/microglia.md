# Microglia

> [!abstract] In one line
> The resident innate-immune macrophages of the central nervous system, central to brain development, homeostasis, and disease.

## Definition
Microglia are the brain's resident myeloid/macrophage population, derived from yolk-sac progenitors and seeding the CNS during early [[development stage|development]]. They surveil the parenchyma, prune synapses, clear debris and pathogens, and shift between functional [[cell state|states]] in response to their microenvironment.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Microglial dysfunction is implicated across [[neurodegenerative disease]], [[neurodevelopmental disease]], and [[neuropsychiatric disease]]. Disease-context states such as [[disease-associated microglia]] (DAM) and processes like [[microglia activation]] and [[neuroinflammation]] are leading research and [[therapeutic targets|therapeutic]] frontiers.

## Key points
- Yolk-sac origin; self-renewing; long-lived.
- Highly [[brain region|region]]- and [[development stage|age]]-dependent heterogeneity, resolved by [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]] and [[spatial transcriptomics]].
- Engage in [[cell-cell interaction]] with neurons, astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, and vasculature.
- Carry many disease-risk genes (e.g. [[gene relationship|GWAS loci]]) enriched in myeloid programs.
