# Gene relationship

> [!abstract] In one line
> Associations linking genes to diseases, cell states, or traits — e.g., risk loci, expression markers, and causal genes.

## Definition
A gene relationship captures how a gene connects to a phenotype or context: GWAS risk associations, marker-gene membership of a [[cell state]], eQTL/colocalization links, or causal evidence. Distinct from [[gene interaction]] (gene–gene functional/physical interplay).

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Many [[Alzheimer's disease]] and other [[neurodegenerative disease]] risk genes are enriched in [[microglia]] (e.g., *TREM2*, *APOE*, *BIN1*, *CR1*), making gene→cell-state→disease relationships central to interpreting genetics.

## Key points
- Sources: GWAS, eQTL/colocalization, single-cell marker analysis, perturbation screens.
- Connects [[gene interaction|gene networks]] to [[biomarkers]] and [[therapeutic targets]].
