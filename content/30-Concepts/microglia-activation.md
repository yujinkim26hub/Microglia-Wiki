# Microglia activation

> [!abstract] In one line
> The transition of [[microglia]] from surveillant/homeostatic states to reactive states in response to perturbation.

## Definition
Microglia activation refers to the morphological, transcriptional, and functional shift of microglia toward reactive [[cell state|states]] — historically (and now critiqued) framed as "M1/M2," increasingly described by data-driven state taxonomies such as [[disease-associated microglia]].

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Activation underlies [[neuroinflammation]] and shapes outcomes across [[neurodegenerative disease]], [[neurodevelopmental disease]], and [[neuropsychiatric disease]].

## Key points
- The binary M1/M2 model is largely superseded by multidimensional [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]] state definitions.
- Triggered through [[cell-cell interaction]] and damage/danger signals.
- Context-, [[brain region|region]]-, and [[development stage|age]]-dependent.
