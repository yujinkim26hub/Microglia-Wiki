# Development stage

> [!abstract] In one line
> The temporal axis of brain development and aging that strongly shapes [[microglia]] identity and function.

## Definition
Development stage refers to the timeline from embryonic colonization through postnatal maturation, adulthood, and aging. Microglia exhibit stage-specific transcriptional programs (e.g., proliferative/amoeboid early states, axon-tract-associated microglia, homeostatic adult states).

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Stage shapes vulnerability windows: developmental microglial roles underlie [[neurodevelopmental disease]], while aging primes microglia toward [[disease-associated microglia]] states relevant to [[neurodegenerative disease]].

## Key points
- Microglial states are age-dependent across the lifespan.
- Interacts with [[brain region]] to produce spatiotemporal heterogeneity.
- Resolved by developmental [[brain single-cell atlas|atlases]] and [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]].
