# Neuropsychiatric disease

> [!abstract] In one line
> Disorders of mood, cognition, and behavior — e.g., schizophrenia, major depression, bipolar disorder — with growing evidence of immune/microglial involvement.

## Definition
Neuropsychiatric diseases include schizophrenia, major depressive disorder, bipolar disorder, and related conditions. They have complex genetic and environmental etiologies and increasingly recognized [[neuroinflammation|neuroinflammatory]] components.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
[[Microglia]] influence synaptic refinement and [[neuroinflammation]]; immune-related [[gene relationship|risk loci]] (e.g., complement in schizophrenia) implicate microglial [[cell-cell interaction]] in psychiatric risk, overlapping with [[neurodevelopmental disease]].

## Key points
- Complement-mediated synaptic pruning linked to schizophrenia risk.
- Inflammatory signatures reported in mood disorders.
