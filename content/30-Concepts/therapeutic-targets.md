# Therapeutic targets

> [!abstract] In one line
> Molecules, pathways, or cell states whose modulation may treat neurological disease.

## Definition
Therapeutic targets are nodes — receptors, enzymes, pathways, or [[cell state|cell states]] — that can be modulated for benefit. Microglia-focused examples include TREM2 agonism, CSF1R modulation, complement (C1q/C3) inhibition, and NLRP3 inflammasome blockade.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Microglia are increasingly viewed as druggable mediators of [[Alzheimer's disease]] and other [[neurodegenerative disease|neurodegenerative]] conditions; targeting [[microglia activation]], [[neuroinflammation]], and [[disease-associated microglia]] states is an active therapeutic strategy.

## Key points
- Target nomination draws on [[gene relationship|genetics]], [[gene interaction|networks]], and [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq|single-cell]] state analysis.
- Paired with [[biomarkers]] for trial readouts.
