# Cell-cell interaction

> [!abstract] In one line
> Communication between cells via ligand–receptor signaling, contact, and secreted factors — shaping microglial behavior in tissue.

## Definition
Cell-cell interactions are the signaling relationships between cells, often inferred computationally from ligand–receptor expression across cell types ([[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]]) and localized in tissue by [[spatial transcriptomics]]. For [[microglia]], key partners include neurons, astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, endothelial cells, and infiltrating immune cells.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Interactions drive [[microglia activation]], [[neuroinflammation]], synaptic pruning, and the emergence of [[disease-associated microglia]]; many [[therapeutic targets]] are interaction nodes (e.g., complement, cytokines, TREM2 ligands).

## Key points
- Inferred via ligand–receptor tools (e.g., CellPhoneDB, CellChat, NicheNet).
- Spatial context is essential to distinguish true neighbors from co-expression artifacts.
- Distinct from [[gene interaction|intracellular gene interactions]].
