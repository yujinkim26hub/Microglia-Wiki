# Cell state

> [!abstract] In one line
> A transient, context-dependent transcriptional/functional configuration of a cell — distinct from fixed cell *type*.

## Definition
A cell state is a reversible, environment-driven configuration of gene expression and function within a cell type. For [[microglia]], states span homeostatic, [[microglia activation|activated]], [[disease-associated microglia|disease-associated]], proliferative, and interferon-responsive programs.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Disease often shifts the *distribution* of microglial states rather than creating new cell types. Resolving states is the core deliverable of [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]] and [[brain single-cell atlas]] work and underpins [[biomarkers]] and [[therapeutic targets]].

## Key points
- State ≠ type: states are dynamic and reversible.
- Defined via clustering, trajectory inference, and marker programs.
- Mapped in tissue context by [[spatial transcriptomics]]; enriched by [[multi-omics]].
