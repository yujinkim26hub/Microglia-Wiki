# Disease-associated microglia (DAM)

> [!abstract] In one line
> A conserved, disease-context microglial [[cell state]] characterized by downregulation of homeostatic genes and upregulation of phagocytic/lipid programs.

## Definition
Disease-associated microglia (DAM), also discussed under related labels (e.g., MGnD, activated response microglia), are a transcriptional state first defined by single-cell profiling of neurodegeneration models. They lose homeostatic markers (e.g., *P2ry12*, *Tmem119*, *Cx3cr1*) and gain phagocytic, lipid-handling, and immune genes (e.g., *Trem2*, *Apoe*, *Cst7*, *Lpl*).

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
DAM are a central concept for [[Alzheimer's disease]] and broader [[neurodegenerative disease]], proposed to cluster around plaques and damaged tissue. They are a leading source of [[therapeutic targets]] and [[biomarkers]].

## Key points
- Defined and refined by [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]] and [[brain single-cell atlas]] efforts.
- Often [[gene relationship|TREM2-dependent]] in part; tightly linked to [[microglia activation]] and [[neuroinflammation]].
- Spatial organization (around pathology) resolved by [[spatial transcriptomics]].
- Cross-species and cross-disease correspondence is an active debate.
