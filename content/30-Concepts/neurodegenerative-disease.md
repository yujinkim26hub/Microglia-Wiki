# Neurodegenerative disease

> [!abstract] In one line
> Disorders marked by progressive loss of neurons and their function, often with protein aggregation and chronic [[neuroinflammation]].

## Definition
Neurodegenerative diseases include [[Alzheimer's disease]], Parkinson's disease, ALS, frontotemporal dementia, and Huntington's disease. They feature progressive neuronal dysfunction/death, characteristic protein pathology, and prominent glial responses.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
[[Microglia]] and [[disease-associated microglia]] states are heavily implicated; microglial [[gene relationship|risk genes]], [[neuroinflammation]], and [[cell-cell interaction]] are central mechanisms and [[therapeutic targets]].

## Key points
- Shared themes: aggregation, chronic inflammation, selective vulnerability by [[brain region]].
- Increasingly dissected with [[snRNA-seq scRNA-seq]], [[spatial transcriptomics]], and [[brain single-cell atlas|atlases]].
