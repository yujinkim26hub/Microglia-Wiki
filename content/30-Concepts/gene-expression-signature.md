# Gene expression signature

> [!warning] Seed note
> This is a **seed page** that needs paper-backed refinement. It will be expanded and cited as papers are ingested.

> [!abstract] In one line
> A characteristic set of genes whose coordinated expression marks a [[cell state|state]], condition, or response — e.g., the [[disease-associated microglia|DAM]] signature.

## Definition
A gene expression signature is a curated group of genes (often from [[differential gene expression]]) whose joint expression distinguishes a biological state. Signatures can be scored in new datasets to classify cells or samples. The DAM program (e.g., *Trem2*, *Apoe*, *Cst7*, *Lpl* up; homeostatic genes down) is a canonical microglial example.

## Why it matters for microglia / neurological disease
Signatures let researchers detect microglial [[cell state|states]] (such as [[disease-associated microglia]]) and [[neuroinflammation]] across studies and tissues, and serve as candidate [[biomarkers]] and entry points to [[therapeutic targets]].

## Key points
- Built from [[differential gene expression]]; applied via module/signature scoring.
- Enable cross-dataset comparison against a [[brain single-cell atlas]].
- Strengthened by [[multi-omics]] and spatial validation ([[spatial transcriptomics]]).
